# DigiWell — App Screens & Features Overview

Here is a simple, plain-English breakdown of exactly what happens on every single screen in the DigiWell application. 

### 1. 🏠 Dashboard (`/dashboard`)
**The Main Hub**: This is your "at-a-glance" screen. It pulls in data from everywhere else to show you a quick summary of today's total screen time, your current wellness score, recent alerts, and brief AI predictions (like if your usage today is unusually high).

### 2. 📊 Analytics (`/analytics`)
**The Deep Dive**: This screen is all about charts and graphs. It visually breaks down exactly where your time went. You can see a donut chart of your most-used apps, a bar chart of what time of day you are most active, and how your screen time trended over the past week.

### 3. 🧠 Predictions (`/predictions`)
**The Crystal Ball**: This uses the Machine Learning models. It looks at your data and predicts your "risk windows" (times you are most likely to doom-scroll). It tells you how your screen time might be affecting your productivity or mental health, and calculates your specific "User Persona" (e.g., "Heavy Scroller" vs "Productive Worker").

### 4. ⏱️ App Tracker (`/tracker`)
**The Live Monitor & Enforcer**: This page watches your live Windows app usage second-by-second. You can view exactly how long you've had windows open today. More importantly, this is where you **set app limits** (e.g., "Limit WhatsApp to 15 mins"). When you hit the limit, it will either warn you, force-close the app, or lock your screen for a mandatory break.

### 5. 🎯 Focus Mode (`/focus`)
**The Distraction Vault**: Built for deep work. You start a countdown timer (e.g., 25 mins) and check off apps that distract you (like Discord or Steam). While the timer is running, if you try to open any of those banned apps, DigiWell instantly shuts them down before they can even load.

### 6. 💬 AI Coach (`/coach`)
**Your Private Therapist**: This is a chat window connected to a completely private, offline AI brain (`Ollama`). The AI is context-aware—it knows exactly how many hours you've spent on your PC today and what your top apps are. You can chat with it to get personalized, realistic advice on how to step away from the screen.

### 7. 📔 Mood Journal (`/journal`)
**The Emotion Tracker**: Screen time affects your brain. This page gives you a daily form to log your current mood, energy levels, and sleep quality. Over time, this helps correlate your high screen-time days to your bad mood or tired days.

### 8. 📄 Reports (`/reports`)
**The Paper Trail**: Once you've gathered enough data, you come here to generate and download beautifully formatted PDF reports. It compiles your screen time history, AI insights, and habits into a single document you could review or share with a therapist.

### 9. 👤 Profile (`/profile`)
**Your Settings**: Where you tell the app who you are. You enter demographic data like your age, profession, and your most-used social media platforms. The Machine Learning models use this background information to make their predictions much more accurate for *you*.

### 10. 🌱 Wellness Tips (`/wellness`)
**The Library**: A helpful list of healthy screen habits, physical eye exercises (like the 20-20-20 rule), and ergonomic tips to help you maintain a healthier relationship with your digital devices.
